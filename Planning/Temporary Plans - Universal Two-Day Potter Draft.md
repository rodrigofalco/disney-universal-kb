# Temporary Plans - Universal Two-Day Potter Draft

## Status
Temporary proposal for review with Rodrigo and Mariangeles. Not locked.

## Context
- Trip has 2 park-to-park Universal days across Universal Studios Florida and Islands of Adventure.
- Early Park Admission is included.
- Goal: use early entry in a different park each day, ride Hogwarts Express in both directions, and avoid a rigid 50/50 split that wastes time.

## Planning goal
Build a practical 2-day approach for the two classic Universal parks that:
- uses one early entry at Islands of Adventure
- uses one early entry at Universal Studios Florida
- treats Hogwarts Express as an attraction plus transition, not mandatory transport at peak wait times
- leaves day 2 flexible for repeats and cleanup

## Proposed plan

### Day 1 - Islands of Adventure first, then cross to Studios

#### Main idea
Start with Early Park Admission at Islands of Adventure, hit the highest-demand attractions there first, enjoy Hogsmeade, then cross by Hogwarts Express to Universal Studios Florida.

#### Morning priorities
1. Hagrid's Magical Creatures Motorbike Adventure
2. Harry Potter and the Forbidden Journey
3. Hogsmeade walk-through
4. Optional if timing and interest allow:
   - Flight of the Hippogriff
   - Jurassic World VelociCoaster

#### Midday transition
- Take Hogwarts Express from Hogsmeade to King's Cross when the posted wait looks reasonable.
- Do not force the train at a bad time just to stay on a rigid schedule.

#### Afternoon at Universal Studios Florida
1. Diagon Alley
2. Harry Potter and the Escape from Gringotts
3. Ollivanders / shops / Butterbeer / photos if desired
4. Non-Potter optional attractions depending on energy and waits:
   - Revenge of the Mummy
   - MEN IN BLACK Alien Attack
   - Transformers
   - Hollywood Rip Ride Rockit if operating and of interest

#### Night
- Finish in Studios unless the day flow changes materially.
- Note what deserves a repeat on day 2.

### Day 2 - Universal Studios first, then cross back to Islands of Adventure

#### Main idea
Start with Early Park Admission at Universal Studios Florida, finish the high-value Studios items early, then ride Hogwarts Express in the opposite direction and use the rest of the day as a flexible cleanup/repeat block.

#### Morning priorities
1. Harry Potter and the Escape from Gringotts if not completed well on day 1 or if worth repeating
2. Diagon Alley with lower early-day crowds
3. Remaining major Studios priorities

#### Midday transition
- Take Hogwarts Express from King's Cross to Hogsmeade.
- This direction is often described by fans as especially strong for a first full reveal, but with 2 days available the better move is simply to do both directions without rushing.

#### Afternoon at Islands of Adventure
Use the rest of the day for any mix of:
- Hagrid's repeat
- VelociCoaster
- Spider-Man
- Hulk
- Jurassic Park River Adventure
- More time in Hogsmeade
- Flexible repeat time in whichever park is winning

#### Night
- Close in the park that feels strongest after day-of conditions.

## Hogwarts Express recommendation
- Ride it at least once in each direction.
- Preferred mindset: attraction plus themed transition.
- Avoid using it as mandatory transportation during peak waits if it starts eating too much park time.

## Why this proposal works
- One early entry in each park
- Hogwarts Express covered both ways
- Day 1 attacks the most sensitive IOA priorities first
- Day 2 acts as recovery space for missed rides, bad waits, or favorites worth repeating
- Better than a rigid half-day split because it keeps decisions responsive to actual crowd conditions

## Short version for chat
- Day 1: start in Islands of Adventure, do Hogsmeade priorities, cross by train to Universal Studios.
- Day 2: start in Universal Studios, do Diagon Alley priorities, cross by train back to Islands of Adventure.
- Keep the second day flexible.

## Notes from quick external research
- Universal official materials indicate Hogwarts Express requires park-to-park admission and the experience differs by direction.
- Fan discussions repeatedly suggest riding both directions and avoiding peak-hour train waits when trying to maximize rides.
- A common fan preference is to experience King's Cross to Hogsmeade as a strong first-time reveal, but this is a preference note, not a hard rule.

- Are they likely to want a slower lunch / shopping block in Diagon Alley or Hogsmeade?
- Should this remain a flexible framework or be converted into a timed step-by-step itinerary?
