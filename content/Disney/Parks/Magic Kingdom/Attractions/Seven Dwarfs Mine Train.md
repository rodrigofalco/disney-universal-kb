# Seven Dwarfs Mine Train

## Type
- Coaster (family roller coaster; swinging mine cars)

## Location
- **Land:** Fantasyland, Magic Kingdom
- Center of Fantasyland; near It's a Small World and Peter Pan's Flight

## Community sentiment score
- **9.4/10** — Sentiment tier: **Elite**
- Basis: public-source consensus reviewed Mar 2026. Planning guidance, not an official rating.

## What it is
- Family coaster through gem-mining caverns themed to Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs. Vehicles sway side to side during the ride. Mix of indoor animated scenes and outdoor track sections. Short ride (~3 min) but very popular.

## Physical characteristics
- **Height requirement:** 38" (97 cm) — lower bar than most coasters; accessible for most adults and older children
- **Intensity:** Mild-to-moderate — gentle speeds, soft forces, swinging cars add novelty not roughness
- **Motion sickness risk:** Low for most guests
- **Water:** No
- **Drop:** Small dips; nothing significant

## Lightning Lane
- **Single Pass only** — not included in Multi Pass
- Approx. price: $11–$15/person (one of the cheaper Single Passes at WDW)
- Less expensive than TRON; still in high demand due to broad family appeal
- On moderate-crowd late-April days, standby can sometimes be managed vs. TRON (which almost always warrants Single Pass or rope drop). Compare waits on the day.

## Strategy notes
- **Adjacency advantage:** Peter Pan's Flight (Multi Pass Tier 1) and It's a Small World (Tier 2) are immediately adjacent in Fantasyland — efficient cluster for Fantasyland coverage in one block.
- If saving budget vs. buying both Single Passes (TRON + Seven Dwarfs): prioritize TRON for Single Pass and attempt Seven Dwarfs via LLMP Tier 2 refresh or standby during a lull.
- Still builds waits early — don't assume you can walk up freely by 9:30 AM.

## Trip relevance
High — one of the most iconic family coasters at Walt Disney World. Clear priority unless you have no interest in family coasters.
