# Early Theme Park Entry

## What is confirmed
Disney's official early-entry page indicates that guests staying at Disney Resort hotels get **30-minute early entry** at Walt Disney World theme parks, including check-in and check-out days, with valid admission required. Pop Century is listed as an eligible Disney Resort hotel.

## What this means for this trip
Since you are staying at [[../Resorts/Disney's Pop Century Resort]], you should plan around early entry on Disney days.

## Practical strategy
- Arrive at the park **before** the early-entry window starts; do not aim to arrive at the exact opening minute.
- Use early entry for the park's highest-friction ride cluster, not for a low-demand warm-up.
- Keep the first 90 minutes disciplined: headliners first, photos/snacks later.
- If using Disney transport, budget for queueing at the transport system itself, not just park security.

## Park-by-park default use
- [[../Parks/Magic Kingdom]]: often best used to knock out one or two major Tomorrowland / Fantasyland priorities early.
- [[../Parks/EPCOT]]: best used to get ahead on major rides before World Showcase pace takes over.
- [[../Parks/Disney's Hollywood Studios]]: one of the most valuable parks for early entry because several top attractions stack waits quickly.
- [[../Parks/Disney's Animal Kingdom]]: very strong early-entry value, especially if the goal is Pandora or an early safari.

## Variable items to verify closer to trip
- Exact early-entry start times
- Which attractions are participating that week
- Whether any headliner has altered access rules

## Operational note
Early entry matters most when paired with a clear first, second, and third attraction plan.
