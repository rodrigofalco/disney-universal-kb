# Disney Dining Reservations

## Booking window

- **60 days in advance** of the dining date.
- **Pop Century resort benefit:** You can book dining for your **entire stay** starting 60 days before your check-in date.
  - Check-in: 18 Apr → first booking day: **17 Feb 2026** (has already passed as of Mar 21, 2026).
- **Current situation (as of 21 Mar 2026):** The 60-day window for each remaining date is open day-by-day. Today you can book through ~20 May; for Apr 21 onward, you are already within the 60-day window.

**Action if no reservations have been made yet:** Book as soon as possible. You are not too late for most restaurants, but the hardest-to-get spots will already have limited availability.

### Booking logistics

- Reservations open at **6:00 AM Eastern**.
- Fan sites recommend being logged into the My Disney Experience app or website at **5:45 AM** — some slots release slightly early.
- Have your party size, date, and restaurant target ready before the clock hits.

## Hardest reservations to get

These fill up fast and require booking at the first possible minute or repeatedly checking for cancellations.

| Restaurant | Park | Why it's hard | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cinderella's Royal Table | Magic Kingdom | Inside the castle; limited covers | Full character breakfast, lunch, dinner |
| Be Our Guest Restaurant | Magic Kingdom | High demand for castle-area table service | Dinner is harder than lunch |

| Topolino's Terrace | Disney's Riviera Resort | Character breakfast; rooftop views | Off-site but popular for Disney Springs/Skyliner access |

**Cancellations:** Worth checking repeatedly 2–4 weeks out and even 1–2 days out. Disney diners frequently cancel.

## Practical dining by park

### Magic Kingdom
- Limited table service compared to other parks.
- **Worth a reservation:** Be Our Guest (dinner), Cinderella's Royal Table (if princess/castle experience matters).
- **Quick service that works:** Columbia Harbour House (Liberty Square; seafood, no characters, indoor seating), Pinocchio Village Haus (Fantasyland, overlooks Small World boat).
- **Skip:** Most Tomorrowland fast food options.

### EPCOT
- Most dining options in all of Walt Disney World. World Showcase has a full-service restaurant in nearly every pavilion.
- **Flower & Garden Festival (your dates):** Outdoor kitchen booths supplement every restaurant. If you want to food-stroll, you don't need a table service reservation.
- **Worth a reservation:** Space 220 (for the experience), Les Chefs de France (solid French food), Tokyo Dining or Teppan Edo (Japan), Via Napoli (pizza, Italy), Coral Reef Restaurant (World Nature, dining overlooking the aquarium).
- **Quick service that works:** Festival booth grazing is itself a meal if you plan 4–6 booth stops.

### Hollywood Studios
- **Worth a reservation:** Oga's Cantina (book in advance), Sci-Fi Dine-In Theater (unique; fun even if food is average), The Hollywood Brown Derby (if you want a proper meal).
- **Avoid:** Docking Bay 7 is quick-service but usually has long waits during lunch peak.
- **If Fantasmic! is in your plan:** Consider dining earlier in the day so the evening is free.

### Animal Kingdom
- **Worth a reservation:** Tiffins (if one splurge AK meal makes sense), Tusker House (character breakfast if characters matter; otherwise skip for efficiency).
- **Quick service that works:** Satu'li Canteen (Pandora; best QS in the park), Harambe Market (good variety), Flame Tree Barbecue (great outdoor seating).
- **Practical note:** AK is often done by mid-afternoon; you may prefer a lighter in-park meal and a proper dinner back at the resort or Disney Springs.

## Disney Springs

- No park ticket required.
- Has the most upscale and varied restaurant options in the Disney World area.
- **Worth a reservation if you have a non-park evening:**
  - The BOATHOUSE
  - Wine Bar George
  - Paddlefish
  - Splitsville Luxury Lanes (food is better than expected)
- Disney Springs is a good choice for the evening of 18 Apr (check-in day) or any lighter day where you do not stay in-park for dinner.

---

## Cancellation policy

- Most restaurants: cancel up to **2 hours before** the reservation without a fee.
- Some signature restaurants: **24-hour** cancellation required to avoid a per-person no-show charge (~$10–$25/person).
- Always check the cancellation policy when booking.
