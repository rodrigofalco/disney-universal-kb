## EPCOT International Flower & Garden Festival

Your entire Disney stay (18–24 Apr) falls within the festival window.

- **Festival dates:** 4 Mar – 1 Jun 2026
- **Included with regular EPCOT admission** — no extra cost
- 20+ outdoor kitchen food booths with small-plate food and drinks (~$5–$10/item)
- Character topiaries throughout World Showcase
- Garden Rocks Concert Series on select evenings (some require separate ticket)

See [[../Parks/EPCOT/Flower-Garden-Festival-2026]] for booth list and strategy.

**Planning implication:** EPCOT during Flower & Garden is materially different from a normal day — the World Showcase becomes a food stroll, not just country pavilions. Build at least 2–3 hours into the EPCOT day for booth grazing if this appeals to you.

## April weather in Orlando

- **Average high:** 83°F (28°C)
- **Average low:** 62°F (17°C)
- **Rain:** April has ~10 rain days on average; brief afternoon thunderstorms are common from mid-afternoon onward
- **Humidity:** Noticeable but not as oppressive as June–August
- **Practical notes:**
  - Carry a light rain layer or small umbrella
  - Afternoon downpours are short — wait them out in an indoor attraction
  - Morning is the most comfortable outdoor window; use it for outdoor queues and safari
  - April is warm enough that water rides feel good by late morning

## Impact on planning

- Drop Big Thunder Mountain from all MK plan versions — it will not be available.
- Drop Rock 'n' Roller Coaster from all DHS plan versions — almost certain it's still closed.
- Add EPCOT Flower & Garden as a feature of your EPCOT day, not just a ride day.
- Crowd levels in late April mean LLMP day-of refresh should be more available than in peak season — but still book LLSP for TRON, Guardians, Rise, and Flight of Passage early.
- Afternoon rain risk: plan indoor-heavy mid-afternoons when possible. Animal Kingdom and Magic Kingdom have the most outdoor queue exposure.
